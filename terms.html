Section 1

What is PAUSE App? 🌱

PAUSE App is a free digital wellness tool created by the Department of Community Medicine at Government Medical College, Maheshwaram. It was built with one simple goal — to help everyday people understand how their digital habits are affecting their health, and to give them practical tools to feel better.

Using internationally validated screening tools, PAUSE helps you check in on six areas of digital health: health-related internet searching, social media use, short-form video habits, gaming, AI tool dependency, and digital work stress. It then gives you personalised, evidence-based guidance to improve.

🏥 Built by community medicine doctors. Our department's mission is the health of the whole community — not just individuals. Every feature in PAUSE App was designed with that public health spirit in mind. This is our gift to the community we serve.

Section 2

Who can use PAUSE App? 🙋

PAUSE App is open to anyone who is 18 years or older and wants to take a friendly, honest look at their digital habits. Whether you're a student, a working professional, a healthcare worker, or simply a curious member of the public — you are welcome here.

The app works on any smartphone or computer with an internet browser and is completely free to use.

💙 If you are going through a very difficult time emotionally right now, we gently suggest speaking to a trusted person or healthcare professional first. PAUSE App is a wellness tool — it's here to support you, but it's not designed for crisis situations.

Section 5

How your information helps the community 🌍

When you use PAUSE App, you are not just helping yourself — you are contributing to something much larger. Your anonymised data helps build a picture of digital health across India that simply does not exist yet, and that could shape public health decisions for years to come.

  • Helps doctors and public health professionals understand how widespread digital disorders are in India
  • Provides evidence to advocate for digital wellness programmes in schools, colleges, and workplaces
  • Contributes to peer-reviewed research that shapes health guidelines and government policy
  • Helps future versions of PAUSE App become even more helpful for people like you

🎓 Your data is used purely for community benefit. It will never be sold, shared with advertisers, or used for any commercial purpose — ever. This is a promise we make as healthcare professionals.

Research findings may be shared at medical conferences or published in health journals — always in aggregated, anonymised form. No one will ever be able to identify you from any published data.

Section 6

How we keep your data safe 🔐

We take the responsibility of holding your information very seriously. Here is what we have done to protect it:

  • All data is stored on Supabase — a secure, modern cloud database with full encryption in transit and at rest
  • Your assessment scores are stored separately from your email address — they cannot be linked back to you in any published analysis
  • Your daily logbook, personal journal entries, and badges stay on your device only — we never see them
  • Only the core research team has any access to the anonymised dataset

🛡️ Your trust means everything to us. We are doctors — protecting the wellbeing of the people we serve is fundamental to who we are, and that includes your data.

Section 8

A note on clinical use 🏥

PAUSE App is designed to be a helpful, honest first step — a friendly way to check in on your digital health using validated tools. It is not a replacement for a consultation with your doctor.

  • Your results give you useful, evidence-based insight — but they are not a clinical diagnosis
  • The CBT tools are general wellness exercises and are not a substitute for formal psychotherapy
  • If your scores suggest a Moderate or Severe level in any area, we warmly encourage you to speak with a healthcare professional

💙 Think of PAUSE as a caring, knowledgeable friend who helps you understand what's going on — and always points you toward the right support when you need it.

Section 10

About our Health Impact Modules 💡

Alongside the six internationally validated disorder screening tools, PAUSE App includes four Health Impact Modules — friendly five-question check-ins that explore how your digital habits may be affecting everyday life in four important areas:

😴
Sleep Disruption
How device use before and during the night affects your sleep quality and daytime energy
🧠
Attention & Focus
How notifications, app-switching, and digital distractions chip away at your ability to concentrate
⚡
Productivity
How digital interruptions affect your work, study, and ability to complete the things that matter to you
❤️
Emotional Health
How constant connectivity, notifications, and social media affect your mood and mental energy

Where the inspiration came from 📚

These modules were thoughtfully developed drawing on a growing body of peer-reviewed research that consistently shows digital habits have real, measurable effects on these four areas of life. Here is the science that inspired each one:

  • Sleep Disruption — inspired by well-established research linking digital device use to delayed bedtimes, night-time waking, and daytime fatigue, extensively studied using the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (Buysse et al., 1989) and more recently the Comprehensive Online Sleep Monitoring Scale (2024), which was specifically designed to capture sleep disruption arising from digital device use.
  • Attention & Focus — inspired by the Smartphone Distraction Scale (Throuvala, Pontes, Griffiths et al., Frontiers in Psychiatry, 2021), a validated 16-item psychometric tool that identifies attention impulsiveness, online vigilance, and the cognitive cost of constant digital switching — validated in over 1,000 participants.
  • Productivity — grounded in research connecting smartphone distraction to real-world performance impairment, drawing on the productivity constructs of the Smartphone Distraction Scale (Throuvala et al., 2021) and the broader Work Productivity and Activity Impairment literature (Reilly et al., 1993).
  • Emotional Health — inspired by the Digital Stress Scale (Hall et al., Psychological Assessment, 2021), a validated 24-item measure covering availability stress, approval anxiety, connection overload, and online vigilance — all of which link patterns of digital use to mood disruption, anxiety, and emotional exhaustion.

💛 An honest note from us: These modules are exploratory wellness check-ins, not formally validated psychometric scales. Their questions reflect the research above, but have not yet undergone independent validation. A formal study is planned. Until then, treat your results as a helpful personal reflection — a starting point for positive change, not a clinical measurement.

🔬 Want to contribute to the science? Every assessment you complete adds to our anonymised dataset. As this dataset grows, it will form the foundation for validating these modules properly — so your participation is genuinely helping turn these exploratory tools into something even more robust and useful for the whole community.
